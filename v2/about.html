People

The people behind the practice.

Seven people who remember your pet's name. One clinic that's stayed the same size, on purpose, for twenty years.

01
The founder

Dr. Nancy
Roncal.

Founder · Veterinarian · Since 2004

Dr. Nancy spent the first fifteen years of her career in high-volume urban animal hospitals — the kind of places where exams are slotted at fifteen-minute intervals and the person who sees your pet this year is not the person who will see your pet next year.

She opened Rathburn in 2004 with a plain goal: run a practice the way she wished she'd been able to practise. Thirty-minute first visits. Written estimates before anything happens. Phone calls answered by someone who was in the room.

Twenty years later, it's still a small clinic. It is still the same doctor in the same building, answering the same phone.

"I'm not trying to run a hospital. I'm trying to run a doctor's office."
02
How we work

Three habits that haven't changed in twenty years.

i.

Take the time.

First visits are thirty to forty-five minutes. Follow-ups are as long as they need to be. No one wants a veterinarian running out of the room mid-thought.

ii.

Tell you the price first.

You'll get a written estimate before anything is done. You'll get an itemized invoice after. No discoveries, no upsells, no "well, as long as we're in there".

iii.

Stay small, stay independent.

No corporate parent, no brand standards, no buyout in the pipeline. When you call, you're reaching a family business — not a call centre.
