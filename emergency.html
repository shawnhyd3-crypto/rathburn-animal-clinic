If your pet is experiencing a medical emergency

Call us immediately at (416) 622-8818

Time matters when your pet is in distress. Our team can advise you on next steps and prepare to see your pet right away if we're open.

Signs Your Pet Needs Emergency Care

If your pet shows any of these signs, seek veterinary care immediately:

  • Severe bleeding or uncontrolled bleeding that won't stop
  • Difficulty breathing, gasping, or choking
  • Seizures or loss of consciousness
  • Suspected poisoning or ingestion of a toxic substance
  • Severe trauma such as hit by a car, serious fall, or attack
  • Inability to walk or complete paralysis
  • Uncontrollable vomiting or diarrhea
  • Inability to urinate or defecate
  • Severe abdominal pain or bloating
  • Difficulty giving birth or suspected pregnancy complications
  • Eye trauma or sudden vision loss
  • Signs of extreme pain or distress

What to Do While Getting to the Clinic

Call Ahead

Contact us immediately at (416) 622-8818 so we can prepare for your arrival and get you the fastest treatment possible.

Keep Your Pet Calm

Speak in a soothing voice. Keep your pet warm with a blanket and minimize movement to prevent further injury.

Manage Bleeding

If there's visible bleeding, apply gentle pressure with a clean cloth or gauze. Do not remove any embedded objects.

Transport Safely

Use a carrier or crate if possible to prevent movement during transport. If your pet can't move, use a board or blanket to support them.

Bring Documents

If available, bring your pet's medical records, vaccination history, and any information about what caused the emergency.

Have Your Information Ready

Know your contact information and pet's age, medical history, and any medications they take so you can provide details quickly.
