Emergency

When your pet needs help now.

During clinic hours we accept same-day urgent appointments. Outside our hours, head to a 24-hour emergency hospital.

During clinic hours: Call us first at (416) 622-8818. We'll advise whether to come in or go directly to emergency.

Is this an emergency?

If your pet shows any of these signs, don't wait. Head to the nearest 24-hour emergency hospital or call us during open hours.

Go now

  • Difficulty breathing or choking
  • Collapse, seizures, unresponsiveness
  • Severe bleeding that won't stop
  • Suspected poisoning (chocolate, xylitol, rodenticide, antifreeze, human medications)
  • Trauma: hit by car, fall, major wound
  • Unable to urinate (especially male cats)
  • Bloated or distended abdomen
  • Heatstroke (collapse, panting, hot to touch)

Call us soon

  • Vomiting or diarrhea lasting more than 24 hours
  • Not eating for more than a day
  • Limping that isn't improving
  • Unusual lumps or swelling
  • Eye discharge or squinting
  • Excessive drinking or urinating
  • Behavior changes (hiding, aggression, confusion)
